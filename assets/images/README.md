# Portfolio Images

This folder contains images for your portfolio website.

## Hero Background Image

Add your hero background image here as `hero-background.jpg`

### Recommended Specifications:
- **Format**: JPG or WebP
- **Resolution**: 1920x1080 or higher (Full HD minimum)
- **Aspect Ratio**: 16:9 for best results
- **File Size**: Under 500KB (compress for web)
- **Style**: Cinematic, dark, high contrast images work best

### Suggestions for Audiovisual Portfolio:
- Behind-the-scenes production shot
- Film equipment close-up (camera, editing setup)
- Color grading workspace
- Cinematic landscape or abstract scene
- Motion blur or dynamic composition

### How to Add:
1. Download or create your hero image
2. Rename it to: `hero-background.jpg`
3. Place it in this folder: `assets/images/`
4. The website will automatically use it!

**Current**: The gradient background acts as a fallback until you add your image.

## Project Thumbnail Images

Add thumbnail images for each of your portfolio projects to help visitors distinguish between them.

### Naming Convention:
- **Project 1**: `project1-thumb.jpg`
- **Project 2**: `project2-thumb.jpg`
- **Project 3**: `project3-thumb.jpg`
- **Project 4**: `project4-thumb.jpg`
- **Project 5**: `project5-thumb.jpg`
- **Project 6**: `project6-thumb.jpg`

### Recommended Specifications:
- **Format**: JPG or WebP
- **Resolution**: 1280x720 (16:9 aspect ratio)
- **File Size**: Under 200KB each (compress for fast loading)
- **Style**: Frame grab or representative shot from the video
- **Quality**: Sharp, well-composed, visually appealing

### Best Practices for Thumbnails:
- Choose a visually striking frame from your video
- Ensure the image clearly represents the project
- Use high contrast and good composition
- Avoid blurry or poorly lit frames
- Consider adding a subtle vignette or color grade for consistency

### How to Create Thumbnails:
1. **From YouTube/Vimeo**: Download thumbnail from video platform
2. **From Video Editor**: Export a frame at highest quality
3. **Screenshot**: Pause video at best frame and capture
4. **Design Tool**: Create custom thumbnail in Photoshop/Canva

### How to Add:
1. Create/export your thumbnail images
2. Name them following the convention above
3. Place all thumbnails in this folder: `assets/images/`
4. The website will automatically load them!

**Current**: Gradient placeholders show until you add thumbnails.
